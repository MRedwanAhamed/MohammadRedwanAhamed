Experimental Tests and Setup

The research was conducted with respect to the published literature. Among the performed experimental investigations in the literature (Ostrander, 1970),some tests were chosen for validation since they satisfied the needed demand and topic. Each test featured a unique beam and column section, as well as plate measurements & material qualities. Except for the bolt shank length, the bolt dimensions remained the same.

Failure Pattern - FEM vrs Experimental

In the instance of Test, the failure mechanism was identified as "Beam Compression Flange Buckle" and "Column Web Buckle" in both the experimental and numerical simulations.

FEM Components with Meshing and Boundary Conditions

The steel beam, column, flush endplate, and bolts were the major components of the joints. The beams, columns, endplates, and bolts were all designed independently and then assembled. The beam, column and endplate were modelled using deformable solid extrusion parts. The bolts were designed with deformable solid revolution parts.

Failure Pattern - FEM vrs Experimental

The failure mode of this test was determined to be "Column Web Buckle," however numerical simulations revealed that the failure mode was also "Buckling of Column Flange."

FEM Beam Column Component's Boundary Conditions

The support criteria were specified and adjusted according to symmetry along the z-axis. By setting U1=0 for all assembled elements, displacement along the x-axis was limited. At the given loading point, a displacement of 5 units (U2 = 5) was provided along the y-axis. Pinned support (U1=U2=U3=0) was defined at 4 units from the beam element's terminal to allow for hinged movement owing to the given displacement.

Model Validation

A comparison was carried out to validate the numerical results with the experimental data obtained from published literature. The results of four beam-column specimens (according to the experimental paper) with endplate connections were reviewed in order to validate the numerical results with the experimental findings. The average difference of 8.3% was obtained between the test results and numerical models. The Moment vs Rotation curves show a decent agreement.
